There's no single right number, but there are honest patterns we see across learners, and they're a better guide than picking whichever course "sounds about right."

## 10 hours: for learners who are nearly there

This suits people who already have real driving experience — previous lessons elsewhere, private practice with a family member, or a lapsed provisional licence from years ago — and just need focused polish, mock tests, and route-specific practice before their test.

## 15–20 hours: the most common starting point

Most learners with some prior experience, but not test-ready confidence, do well with 15 to 20 hours. Our 20-hour course is the most booked for exactly this reason — enough time to genuinely build test-day confidence without paying for hours you don't need.

## 30 hours: starting from scratch

If you're a complete beginner, 30 hours gives a realistic, unrushed path from "never driven before" to test-ready, covering everything from basic control to independent driving and manoeuvres, without cramming.

## What actually determines the number

Not age, not how "naturally confident" you feel beforehand — mainly how much genuine seat time you've already had. An honest instructor assessment in your first lesson is worth more than guessing from a course description, and we'll always tell you if we think you've picked the wrong length.
