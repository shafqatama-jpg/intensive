Tolworth gives its name to this course for a reason — it's the test centre our instructors know best, down to which approach roads examiners tend to favour and where nerves usually spike on the Tolworth Roundabout.

Local knowledge that actually matters

The A3 Kingston Bypass runs right past Tolworth, and the roundabout system around it is one of the more complex pieces of road your test is likely to include. Rather than meeting it for the first time on test day, our intensive courses put you through it repeatedly in the days beforehand, at the times of day you're likely to test.

Built for Tolworth's test routes

Examiners from the Tolworth centre draw from a defined set of local routes — residential streets around Berrylands and Hook, the A3 slip roads, and the roundabout itself. Our instructors teach these specifically, not just general driving skill, in the run-up to your test.

The test centre itself is Douglas House, 1b Douglas Road, Tolworth, KT6 7RZ, tucked just off Tolworth Broadway next to Alexandra Park.

The four spots that catch learners out

Every test centre has a handful of junctions that come up again and again. Around Tolworth, these are the ones we drill specifically:

  • Hook Junction. Where the A3 meets the A243 and A309 a couple of miles south of Tolworth — multiple lanes merging at speed, and easy to get caught in the wrong one if you haven't practised it.
  • The Scilly Isles. A genuine double roundabout on the A307/A309 near Hinchley Wood, between Esher and Kingston. It's a well-known local landmark for being awkwardly laid out — the lane discipline through it trips up even confident drivers the first few times.
  • Claygate. Narrower village roads, including The Avenue, where speed limits drop and hazard awareness matters more than raw driving skill.
  • Long Ditton. Quieter residential streets near the Portsmouth Road corridor — deceptively easy-looking, but exactly where an examiner will check you haven't switched off after a busy main road.

None of these are optional extras in our courses — if you're testing from Tolworth, you'll drive all four before test day, not just hear about them.

Learners living in or near Tolworth, Berrylands, Hook, and Surbiton who want their test-centre routes covered properly — whether you're starting from scratch with a 30-hour course or brushing up with a 10-hour top-up before a test you've already booked.
