This is usually one of the first questions we get, and there's no universally "right" answer — it depends on what you'll actually drive afterwards and how you learn best.

## The practical difference

A manual licence lets you drive both manual and automatic cars. An automatic licence only lets you drive automatics. That's the single biggest factor for most people — if there's any chance you'll need to drive a manual car in future (a family car, a work vehicle, driving abroad), it's worth learning manual even if it takes a little longer to feel confident.

## Why some learners choose automatic

Without gear changes and a clutch to manage, automatic lessons let you focus entirely on road positioning, hazard awareness and observation from lesson one. For nervous learners, or anyone who has struggled with clutch control in a manual car, this can make the difference between an intensive course working well and feeling overwhelming.

## Does it change how many hours you need?

Not dramatically, but automatic learners often need slightly fewer hours to reach test standard, simply because there's one less skill to coordinate. It's still worth being honest with your instructor about your experience level rather than assuming automatic means "faster" by default.
