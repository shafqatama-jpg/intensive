Inside the Tolworth Driving Test: Routes, Roundabouts and What to Expect

10 July 2026 · 5 min read

If your test is booked at Tolworth, here's what's actually useful to know beyond "practice a lot" — where the centre is, which junctions come up again and again, and what learners who've been through it recently say about it.

The test centre itself

Tolworth Driving Test Centre is at Douglas House, 1b Douglas Road, Tolworth, KT6 7RZ — just off Tolworth Broadway, next to Alexandra Park. It serves Tolworth, Surbiton, Kingston upon Thames, New Malden, Chessington, Thames Ditton, Esher and Claygate.

The junctions that come up most

  • Tolworth Roundabout — the A3/A240 junction right by the test centre, grade-separated and busy at most times of day.
  • Hook Junction — where the A3 meets the A243 and A309 a couple of miles south, often just called "the Hook roundabout" locally.
  • The Scilly Isles — a genuine double roundabout on the A307/A309 near Hinchley Wood, between Esher and Kingston, known for confusing lane discipline.
  • Claygate and Long Ditton — quieter village and residential roads where examiners check you haven't switched off after a busy main road.

Nationally, it's not an easy test
