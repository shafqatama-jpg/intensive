The Full DVSA 'Show Me, Tell Me' Question List, Explained

5 June 2026 · 6 min read

Two vehicle safety questions come up on every UK practical test — one "tell me" question before you drive off, and one "show me" question while you're driving. Getting one wrong is a single minor fault, not an automatic fail (unless a "show me" answer involves an unsafe action).

The short version

There are 14 possible "tell me" questions and 7 possible "show me" questions. The examiner picks one of each. You can't predict which one, so the only real strategy is knowing all of them well enough that none of them are a surprise.

Where to find the full list

We've published the complete official list — all 14 "tell me" questions with their full answers, and all 7 "show me" questions — on our free learner resources page, sourced directly from GOV.UK and the DVSA under the Open Government Licence.

How to actually learn them

Reading the list once isn't enough. We build these into lessons naturally — asking a "tell me" question while you're doing your normal pre-drive checks, and a "show me" question at a safe, natural moment during the drive, so answering them stops feeling like a memory test and starts feeling like something you'd just do anyway.

The bigger picture

These two questions are a small part of the test, but they're one of the few parts you can fully prepare for in advance with zero ambiguity. There's no reason to lose marks here.
